Adventure in Guatemala
Part 1
My name is Pedro, and I live in a quiet town in Guatemala.
One afternoon, my friend Juan came over with something in his hand. It was a map.
The paper looked old, like it had been hidden for a long time.
Faded lines ran across it, leading into the forest.
We studied it together, trying to understand what it meant.
"Maybe it leads to something important," Juan said. I agreed.
The next morning, we packed a few things and left before the sun was high.
The road was long and hot. We passed trees, birds, and small rivers.
Most of the time, the only sounds were our steps and the wind.
Still, I felt something in the air. The forest seemed quiet—but in a different way, like it was waiting for us.
After hours of walking, we reached a cave.
From the outside, it didn’t look special.
But when I stepped inside, the air turned cold, and the light from the entrance didn’t follow me far.
The walls were covered in pictures. Some showed people, others strange shapes.
One looked like the sun and moon together. Another showed three people with something glowing around them.
I couldn’t explain it, but I felt a strong emotion. It wasn’t fear—more like a heavy feeling in my chest.
Something about the pictures felt personal, like they were part of a story I had forgotten.
As the day ended, we began to walk back. But the forest had changed.
The air was colder. The wind was louder. The trees didn’t feel the same.
Then we heard a sound behind us. Juan looked at me. We both stopped walking.
"I don’t think we’re alone," he said.
From the shadows, an old man stepped out.
His face was serious, and he wore long clothes that moved in the wind.
At first, he said nothing. Then he looked at us and spoke.
"You were inside the cave." We stayed silent.
He nodded. "Be careful. That place holds more than pictures."
Without another word, he turned and walked away into the dark.
We stood there for a moment. I looked at Juan. He looked back at me.
Something had changed. This was only the beginning.
